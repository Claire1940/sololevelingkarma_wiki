<Section icon={Swords} title="How the Run Loop and Combat Flow Work">

The combat loop is simple on paper but demanding in practice. You enter a run, fight through enemies, collect upgrades, and shape a build that can survive later encounters. The best early habit is to make every decision support the next fight, not just the current one.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open With a Stable Plan">
    Pick a weapon direction before you start pushing damage. A stable opening keeps your first upgrades aligned instead of random.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Read Enemy Windows">
    Watch attack tells, then move. Most early losses come from staying in range too long or spending mobility too early.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spend Upgrades With Intent">
    Choose upgrades that reinforce the same combat style. If you lean into burst, reinforce burst. If you lean into control, keep control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Review What Ended the Run">
    After each fail, identify the real issue: bad spacing, weak synergy, or poor burst timing. That is where your next improvement starts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Movement | **Learn dodge timing** | Most mistakes come from greedy attacks |
| Weapon identity | **Stick to one style** | Cleaner decisions, better upgrade choices |
| Blessing choice | **Pick upgrades that stack** | Mixed stats usually weaken your build |
| Shadow usage | **Save support for danger** | Helps stabilize elite and boss fights |
