<Section icon={Target} title="How to Start Your First Run">

Your first few runs should be about learning rhythm, not proving damage output. The most efficient early-game mindset is to keep your build simple, track which options feel reliable, and avoid forcing a synergy that only works on paper.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick one comfort range">
    Start with either close-range pressure or safer distance play. A clear range preference makes dodging, spacing, and combo timing easier to read.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Learn one reliable attack pattern">
    Build around a basic loop you can repeat under stress. If a combo only works in perfect conditions, it is too fragile for early runs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spend upgrades on consistency">
    Choose upgrades that improve survival, mobility, or stable damage before chasing niche bonuses that depend on rare drop combinations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Review what failed">
    After each run, identify whether the problem was positioning, timing, or upgrade selection. Fixing one mistake at a time produces faster progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="First-Run Rule">
If a choice feels flashy but unreliable, skip it. In roguelite play, consistent clears usually come from stable habits, not from risky highlight builds.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons, Blessings, and Shadow Army Synergy">

The strongest early habit is to treat weapons, Blessings, and the Shadow Army as one system rather than three separate features. When those pieces support one another, your runs feel smoother. When they clash, you end up spending too much time covering weaknesses instead of pushing damage.

<Callout type="warning" title="Build Mistake to Avoid">
Do not lock into a build just because one part looks strong. A great weapon with poor Blessing support can feel worse than a simpler setup with cleaner synergy.
</Callout>

| System | Main Job | Best Use | Common Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weapons | Core damage and combat range | Direct combat flow | Overcommitting to a single range |
| Blessings | Run-to-run scaling | Tailoring a build to the moment | Taking upgrades that do not connect |
| Shadow Army | Pressure and battlefield control | Creating safer attack windows | Relying on summons to solve everything |
| Boss Knowledge | Pattern reading | Surviving hard encounters | Greedy damage during dangerous phases |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Build Priorities That Make Runs Safer">

If you want your early clears to feel smoother, organize your decisions by priority. In a roguelite structure, the first upgrade you choose often influences the rest of the run more than you expect. That is why you should favor stability before specialization.

<Callout type="success" title="Practical Priority Order">
Start with survival, then reliable damage, then mobility, and only then chase perfect synergy. This order keeps your build functional when the run offers awkward options.
</Callout>

| Priority | Focus | Why It Comes First |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Survival tools | Keeps the run alive during mistakes |
| 2 | Reliable damage | Lets you clear rooms without stalling |
| 3 | Mobility | Makes boss telegraphs easier to answer |
| 4 | Synergy picks | Improves a build that already works |
| 5 | Utility extras | Good bonus value, but rarely the first pick |

| Build Pattern | Simple Goal | Good For |
|---|---|---|
| Direct pressure | Hit hard and keep moving | Players who prefer aggressive pacing |
| Controlled spacing | Attack from safer angles | Players who value survival |
| Flexible switching | Adapt to enemy type | Runs with mixed enemy pressure |
| Summon support | Let allies create openings | Encounters with heavy screen control |

A useful editor’s guideline is to avoid overvaluing “perfect” combinations before you understand the encounter itself. If a Blessing improves your ability to dodge, reposition, or recover from an error, it is often more valuable than a flashy damage bonus.
