<Section icon={Target} title="Your First Four Runs">

Early progress should feel deliberate. The goal is not to clear everything on your first attempt; the goal is to learn how Solo Leveling KARMA rewards timing, spacing, and disciplined upgrade choices.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the movement loop">
    Practice dodging, repositioning, and staying alive under pressure. A clean escape is often more valuable than one extra hit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose one weapon family">
    Stick to a close-range or long-range path for a few runs so you can learn attack timing without changing your rhythm every fight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Take matching Blessings">
    Pick upgrades that reinforce your current build. If your weapon favors burst damage, avoid Blessings that only help slow setups.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Review every failed run">
    Identify whether you lost to greed, bad spacing, or poor upgrade choices, then fix that one problem next attempt.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons, Blessings, and Shadow Army Basics">

The strongest beginner builds in a roguelite usually come from clarity. Pick one combat identity, then stack everything around it. In Solo Leveling KARMA, that means weapon choice, Blessing selection, and Shadow Army timing should all point in the same direction.

| Weapon Path | Best Use | Strength | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sword-type weapons | Close-range pressure | Fast melee hits, direct control | Requires staying close |
| Long-range weapons | Safe damage | Better spacing and safer pokes | Weak if you mismanage distance |
| Special weapons | Flexible builds | Strong synergy with different setups | Depends on good build choices |

| Blessing Focus | What It Does | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Skill Enhancement | Improves combat skill value | Players who like burst windows |
| Stat Growth | Raises core combat power | Safer, steadier progression |
| Build Diversity | Encourages different run outcomes | Players who like replay variety |
| Weapon Synergy | Supports specific weapon paths | Focused build planning |
| Shadow Army Synergy | Boosts summon-based pressure | Players who want stronger combo play |

Practical loadout rules for new players:

- Commit to one weapon style for several runs.
- Prioritize Blessings that match your current pace.
- Use the Shadow Army to extend control, not to cover weak fundamentals.
- Avoid mixing too many unrelated bonuses in one run.
- Favor upgrades that improve survival if boss patterns still feel unfamiliar.

<Callout type="warning" title="Beginner Trap">
Do not build around every strong-looking option at once. Too much variety can weaken your damage curve and make each run feel inconsistent.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Prep and Failure Recovery">

Boss encounters are where good habits become visible. Cerberus, Hobgoblin Leader, Avaricious Vulcan, Demonic King Baran, and the Statue of God all reward patience, spacing, and a clear plan. The safest approach is to learn each threat pattern before trying to force a fast kill.

| Boss | Main Threat | Safer Response |
|---|---|---|
| Cerberus | Wide fire pressure and heavy melee | Save dodges for big area attacks |
| Hobgoblin Leader | Adds and close-range pressure | Control space before committing |
| Avaricious Vulcan | Large hits and area damage | Play for timing, not greed |
| Demonic King Baran | High-level burst and punishing windows | Wait for clear openings |
| Statue of God | Massive survival checks | Prioritize movement and spacing |

A solid boss routine looks like this:

- Enter the fight with a build that already works in normal rooms.
- Keep one defensive habit for every offensive habit you use.
- Spend burst only when the boss is locked in a recoverable animation.
- Preserve room to move; crowded fights usually create avoidable damage.
- If you fail, note the attack that forced the mistake and adjust the next run.

<Callout type="info" title="Boss Mindset">
The best boss prep is consistency. When your movement and upgrade path stay stable, each encounter becomes easier to read and less punishing.
</Callout>
