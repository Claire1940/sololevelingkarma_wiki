<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Combat Loop and Early-Run Priorities">

The combat loop is built for momentum. You enter a run, adapt to the stage, swap weapons when the situation changes, and use Blessings to push the build in a specific direction. The Shadow Army adds another layer of pressure and support, so the best play is usually the one that keeps your options open.

A clean beginner plan is less about memorizing a perfect route and more about learning how each system feeds the next. If you understand how weapon choice changes spacing, and how Blessings amplify that choice, your early runs become much more stable.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the rhythm of a run">
    Focus on movement, enemy tells, and safe windows. The early goal is not speed; it is learning how the game asks you to commit and disengage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Pick a weapon profile">
    Decide whether you want close-range pressure, safer distance control, or a flexible hybrid. Build around that decision instead of changing direction every room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stack Blessings with purpose">
    Choose upgrades that support the same combat plan. A strong Blessing is useful, but a strong synergy is better.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Use Shadow support on demand">
    Treat the Shadow Army as a combat layer, not a visual bonus. Save their value for pressure windows, burst phases, or boss recovery openings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| System | Role in a Run | Player Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Weapons | Damage profile and spacing | Swap based on enemy range and risk |
| Blessings | Build direction | Prioritize synergy over random gains |
| Shadow Army | Support and pressure | Use them to control difficult moments |
| Roguelite progression | Run-to-run adaptation | Improve execution and build judgment |

<Callout type="info" title="Early-Run Rule">

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Weapons and Blessings: Build the Right Loadout">

This is where Solo Leveling KARMA should get most of its long-term replay value. Weapons define your combat texture, while Blessings decide how far that texture can stretch. The smartest loadout is usually not the flashiest one; it is the one that keeps your damage, survival, and utility aligned.

Think in archetypes. A melee-first build needs different support than a ranged setup, and a Shadow-heavy route should not be treated like a pure burst build. The more clearly you define your role, the easier it becomes to choose upgrades during a run.

<Callout type="success" title="Build Rule">
Match your weapon choice to your Blessings. If your upgrades push one direction and your weapon pushes another, your run will usually feel weaker than it should.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Boss Prep: Cerberus, Baran, and High-Risk Encounters">

Boss fights are where sloppy habits get exposed. The current boss lineup centers on iconic threats such as Cerberus, Hobgoblin Leader, Avaricious Vulcan, Demonic King Baran, and the Statue of Cartenon Temple. Each one demands a different answer, but the common rule stays the same: learn the pattern, do not panic, and never spend your safest option too early.

The best prep is mechanical. If you can recognize a big swing, a wide-area attack, or a summon phase before it lands, you will save resources for the part of the fight that actually matters.

<Callout type="warning" title="Boss Prep Warning">
Do not treat every boss as a damage race. Many of these encounters punish greedy melee trades, late dodges, and careless cooldown use more than low raw damage.
</Callout>

| Boss | Main Threat | Good Prep | Common Mistake |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cerberus | Wide fire attacks and heavy melee pressure | Save dodges for big swings and reposition often | Staying too close for too long |
| Hobgoblin Leader | Adds and crowd chaos | Clear support enemies first | Focusing only on the leader |
| Avaricious Vulcan | Large-area damage and heavy hits | Keep moving and punish recovery frames | Overcommitting after every dodge |
| Demonic King Baran | High-level burst patterns | Learn the tells before spending burst skills | Dumping cooldowns too early |
| Statue of Cartenon Temple | Survival check with large-scale attacks | Prioritize spacing and patience | Standing still and trading hits |

A strong boss habit is to enter each fight with a simple three-part plan: stay alive, identify the dangerous pattern, then spend burst only when the arena is safe. That plan is boring, but it is effective.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Watch the first attack cycle">
    Spend the opening moments learning range, speed, and whether the boss prefers area damage or direct pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Protect your emergency tools">
    Keep at least one dodge, defensive window, or summon timing ready for the most dangerous part of the fight.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Punish recovery, not chaos">
    Attack after a predictable animation ends. That is where reliable damage usually comes from.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reset and repeat">
    If the fight gets messy, step back mentally and return to the pattern instead of forcing a bad trade.
  </Step>
</Steps>
