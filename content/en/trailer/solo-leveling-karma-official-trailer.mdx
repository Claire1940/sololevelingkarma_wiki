<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Loadout: Weapons, Switches, and Blessings">

Combat in Solo Leveling KARMA is built to reward timing and adaptation. If you want consistent results, start with a loadout that can survive messy encounters before chasing aggressive damage numbers.

<Callout type="warning" title="Build Warning">
Do not lock yourself into pure offense too early. In roguelite action games, a fragile build can collapse after one bad dodge, one missed swap, or one boss pattern you have not learned yet.
</Callout>

| Weapon Type | Best Use | Strength | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Melee weapons** | Close-range pressure | Strong burst and direct control | Requires risky positioning |
| **Ranged weapons** | Safer spacing | Lets you punish from distance | Lower room for mistakes in tight arenas |
| **Special weapons** | Mixed setups | Synergy-friendly and flexible | Depends on the rest of the build |
| **Swap-driven kits** | Mid-fight adaptation | Good against varied enemy waves | Needs practice and timing |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose a stable opening weapon">
    Start with the weapon style that matches your comfort zone. If you are learning enemy patterns, survival and reach matter more than style points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add one defensive layer">
    Look for any Blessing or upgrade that improves sustain, recovery, or damage reduction. That gives you room to learn boss timing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build one damage engine">
    Once survival is stable, stack effects that enhance your main damage source. Keep your setup focused instead of spreading bonuses too thin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Practice weapon swaps on trash enemies">
    Use weaker fights to train clean transitions. The goal is to make swapping feel automatic before the run reaches high-pressure encounters.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Build Priority | Early Game | Mid Game | Late Game |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Defense** | High | Medium | Medium |
| **Mobility** | High | High | Medium |
| **Burst damage** | Medium | High | High |
| **Synergy stacking** | Low | Medium | High |

<Callout type="info" title="Loadout Rule of Thumb">
A good early build answers three questions: Can I survive the next room, can I clear waves quickly, and can I still fight a boss without panic?
</Callout>

The strongest habit is to treat every upgrade as part of a chain. If one Blessing improves skill damage and another improves pressure after a dodge, those two choices should support the same plan, not fight each other.
