<Section icon={Swords} title="Step-by-Step Run Plan for Early Success">

The first few runs should be about building habits that survive bad RNG. Do not lock yourself into a fragile plan too early. A steady opening rhythm will help you learn enemy spacing, weapon timing, and when to spend resources instead of hoarding them.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid This Mistake">
Do not pick upgrades only because they look rare. In a roguelite, the best choice is often the one that fits your current route, not the one with the flashiest numbers.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open With Movement Discipline">
    Learn dodge timing, attack recovery, and spacing before you try to brute-force damage. Clean movement creates room for better decisions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Your Weapon Identity">
    Commit to a melee, ranged, or hybrid style early enough to make upgrade choices coherent. A focused loadout is easier to scale.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Build Around Synergy">
    Choose Blessings, attack modifiers, and Shadow Army support that reinforce the same combat plan instead of spreading power thin.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Save Power for Boss Windows">
    Preserve your strongest burst, defensive tools, and summon support for dangerous phases instead of spending everything on trash fights.
  </Step>

| Phase | Goal | What to Prioritize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening rooms | Stability | Movement, timing, safe clears |
| Mid-run | Synergy | Matching Blessings to weapon type |

If a run feels messy, tighten the next one around a single combat identity. Most early losses come from mixed priorities, not from low damage alone.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Weapon and Loadout Priorities">

Weapon choice should match how you want to handle space. Solo Leveling KARMA is built for fast adjustments, but that does not mean every weapon should be treated equally in every run. A good loadout is one that improves your ability to control distance, punish openings, and survive mistakes.

<Callout type="info" title="Loadout Rule">
Pick the weapon that solves your current problem. If you need safer clears, choose control. If you already have control, choose burst.
</Callout>

| Weapon Type | Best For | Risk Level | Recommended Mindset |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sword-type weapons | Close-range pressure | Medium | Stay aggressive, but respect recovery frames |
| Long-range weapons | Safer boss practice | Low | Keep spacing clean and punish openings |
| Special weapons | Flexible builds | Medium | Build around synergy, not autopilot |
| High-speed options | Fast room clears | Medium-High | Win by tempo and execution |

A practical early loadout usually needs one clear damage plan and one answer to panic situations. That means your weapon should cover either burst or safe control, while Blessings and summons fill the gap.

<Callout type="tip" title="Practical Loadout Check">
If your weapon only feels good when enemies stand still, it is too narrow for a roguelite run.
</Callout>

| Slot | Good Pick | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main weapon | Reliable damage tool | Keeps your run consistent |
| Support choice | Range or utility | Helps you recover bad rooms |
| Shadow support | Defensive or burst summon | Covers openings and boss pressure |

Do not overvalue a single high-damage tool if it leaves you exposed in multi-wave fights. The strongest loadouts usually combine one dependable core weapon with a system that gives you breathing room.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Blessings, Shadows, and Build Synergy">

Blessings should shape your build instead of simply inflating numbers. Think of them as the engine that turns an ordinary weapon into a run-defining setup. The best combinations usually improve one clear plan: stronger skill damage, better survivability, or more reliable pressure during boss windows.

<Callout type="success" title="Best Build Principle">
Choose Blessings that sharpen your strongest habit. If you win by spacing, enhance control. If you win by pressure, enhance burst.
</Callout>

| Build Focus | Strong Blessing Direction | Shadow Army Role | Common Weak Point |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Burst damage | Skill amplification | Extra pressure during openings | Poor endurance |
| Safe control | Defensive or spacing support | Covering risky lanes | Slower clears |
| Hybrid scaling | Mixed stat gains | Flexible frontline help | Less peak damage |
| Boss breaker | Sustained output boosts | Staggering and distraction | Weak room speed |

The Shadow Army is not just flavor. In practical play, summons can help you hold space, punish enemy attention, and create a window for your strongest attack sequence. Use them as a tactical layer, not as a passive bonus.

| Situation | Best Response | Why It Helps |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Small enemy packs | Fast clearing tool | Keeps momentum high |
| Elite enemy | Controlled burst | Reduces exposure time |
| Boss opening | Summon plus burst | Maximizes pressure window |
| Recovery phase | Defensive spacing | Resets your rhythm |

If a Blessing choice does not improve your current run plan, skip it. Synergy is more valuable than novelty when the fight tempo starts climbing.

</Section>
