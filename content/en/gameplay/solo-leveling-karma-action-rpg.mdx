<Section icon={Target} title="First-Run Setup: Build Habits That Stick">

Your first few runs should be about control, not perfection. The game rewards players who read attack patterns, swap weapons with purpose, and choose Blessings that support the build they already started.

Use the opening phase to remove friction. If a choice makes the run harder to pilot, skip it unless the payoff is obvious.

<Callout type="warning" title="Beginner Trap">
Do not force one “best” weapon path on every run. The roguelite structure is built to punish rigid habits.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Learn the opening flow">
    Focus on movement, basic attacks, and when to disengage. Clean dodges matter more than greedy damage in early encounters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Pick one weapon identity">
    Start with a melee or ranged preference and stick to it long enough to learn its rhythm before mixing in more complex options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Use Blessings to reinforce the plan">
    Choose upgrades that improve the way you already play instead of chasing random power that does not fit the run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Treat every death as data">
    Re-run the same content with one adjustment at a time so you can isolate what actually improved survival.
  </Step>
</Steps>
