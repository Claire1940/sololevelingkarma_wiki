Boss encounters should be approached like pattern tests, not damage races. The official trailers highlight iconic threats such as Cerberus, Baran, the Statue of God, and other high-pressure enemies, which means movement discipline matters as much as damage output.

<Callout type="success" title="Boss-Fight Goal">
The best boss prep is calm execution: learn the pattern, keep your spacing clean, and spend burst only after a safe opening appears.
</Callout>

| Boss | Main pressure | Best response | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cerberus | Fire zones and aggressive melee | Move early, do not stay static | High |
| Hobgoblin Leader | Adds plus close-range pressure | Clear adds and keep space | High |
| Avaricious Vulcan | Heavy area damage | Wait for safe burst windows | High |
| Demonic King Baran | High-level boss pressure | Punish only after clear commits | Very High |

A clean boss plan usually follows three rules: keep an escape route open, avoid burning all cooldowns at once, and stop attacking the moment your positioning gets messy. That discipline matters more than raw aggression in early progression.

| Pre-boss habit | Benefit |
|---|---|
| Save burst | Better punish windows |
| Keep distance options | Safer repositioning |
| Watch attack tells | Cleaner dodges |
| Reset between phases | Lower risk of panic |
