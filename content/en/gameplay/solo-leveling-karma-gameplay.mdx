<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Combat Loop and Run Decisions">

The combat loop is built around fast engagement, flexible weapon swapping, and layered progression inside each run. That means you are not just learning attacks; you are learning when to commit, when to disengage, and when to let your companions and summoned Shadows absorb pressure.

The cleanest way to think about the game is simple: every fight is a resource check. If you spend too much too early, boss phases become messy. If you hold too much back, your damage window disappears.

| System | What it does | Editor takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Weapon switching | Changes your attack profile during combat | Build for flexibility, not one trick |
| Blessings | Add run-specific power and synergy | Stack bonuses around one clear plan |
| Shadow Army | Adds allied pressure and support | Use summons to create safe openings |
| Roguelite progression | Resets power between runs | Learn patterns, then refine the build |

<Callout type="warning" title="Run Planning Matters">
Do not treat each attempt like a normal hack-and-slash mission. solo leveling karma rewards players who enter a run with a purpose, then adjust only when the run gives them a better branch.
</Callout>

A solid combat mindset should focus on three priorities:

- **Spacing**: stay close enough to punish openings, but far enough to avoid wide boss swings.
- **Tempo**: rotate into your strongest skills when the enemy is recovering, not when it is already attacking.
- **Synergy**: pair your weapon choice with Blessings that reinforce the same combat plan.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Weapons and Blessings Build Picks">

Weapons and Blessings define how a run feels moment to moment. Official showcase material points to multiple weapon styles, and the safest read is that each style supports a different rhythm. Some players will want direct pressure, while others will want room to reposition and wait for the right opening.

The best early habit is to choose one primary lane and one backup lane. That keeps your build from becoming too scattered while still giving you options when a run drops the wrong upgrade path.

| Weapon type | Best use | Trade-off |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sword-type | Close-range pressure and fast trades | Higher risk if spacing slips |
| Long-range | Safer damage and battlefield control | Needs disciplined positioning |
| Special/flexible | Build experimentation and combo play | Depends heavily on synergy |

<Callout type="success" title="Best Build Rule">
Pick a weapon style that matches your reflexes, then let Blessings amplify that style instead of fighting it.
</Callout>

| Blessing focus | What to prioritize | Best pairing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Skill growth | Damage, cooldown value, execution power | Sword or special weapons |
| Stat growth | Offensive power and survival room | Balanced loadouts |
| Build diversity | Synergy bonuses and adaptive choices | Experimental runs |
| Shadow synergy | Ally support and combo uptime | Mobile, aggressive setups |

A practical build ladder looks like this:

- **Early runs**: test one melee-leaning and one ranged-leaning setup.
- **Mid runs**: watch which Blessings repeat with good results.
- **Late runs**: lock in your strongest synergy and stop over-editing the build.
- **Boss runs**: keep one defensive answer available, even if your damage is excellent.
