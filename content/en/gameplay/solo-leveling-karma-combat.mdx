<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons and Loadouts">

Weapons define your rhythm, but they do not define the whole run. The best loadout is the one that gives you answers: a way to pressure enemies up close, a way to handle awkward ranges, and a backup plan when a room gets messy. If a weapon looks strong but leaves you exposed, it will cost more runs than it wins.

| Weapon Type | Best For | Risk Level | Best Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sword-type | Close-range pressure | Medium | Fast melee chains and direct trades |
| Dual blades / daggers | Burst and mobility | High | Aggressive routes with quick exits |
| Bow / ranged option | Safer spacing | Low | Controlling dangerous enemies from afar |
| Greatsword / hammer style | Heavy stagger | Medium | Punishing slow targets and elites |
| Scythe / special weapon | Flexible builds | Medium | Runs built around synergy and reach |

| Loadout Slot | Good First Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Main weapon | Fast, reliable damage | Keeps your core loop simple |
| Secondary weapon | Opposite range option | Fixes bad matchups |
| Mobility tool | Dash or escape option | Prevents avoidable deaths |
| Flex slot | Burst or crowd control | Helps with elites and bosses |

<Callout type="warning" title="Loadout Rule">
Do not build around a single flashy weapon. A run fails when it has no answer to ranged enemies, fast swarms, or a sudden boss pressure spike.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Blessings and Run-Build Planning">

Blessings are the real build engine in Solo Leveling KARMA. They shape how your damage scales, how safe your run feels, and how much room you have to recover from bad room layouts. The most efficient approach is to pick one lane early, then keep choosing upgrades that reinforce that lane.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose your main damage lane">
    Decide whether the run is melee-focused, burst-focused, or ranged-focused. A clear lane keeps every later choice easier.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add one safety layer">
    Pick a Blessing or upgrade that reduces risk. Mobility, healing, or damage reduction can save a run that would otherwise collapse.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stack synergy, not variety">
    The strongest runs usually come from repeated bonuses that support the same plan. Keep your upgrades aligned with your weapon choice.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Re-evaluate after elite fights">
    If the run starts drifting, adjust your next picks instead of forcing the original idea. Adaptation is part of the roguelite loop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Blessing Goal | Typical Effect | When to Pick It |
|---|---|---|
| Damage boost | Raises burst or sustained output | When your weapon is already stable |
| Survival boost | Improves durability or recovery | When rooms are punishing |
| Speed boost | Improves mobility or tempo | When you need cleaner positioning |
| Utility boost | Adds control or flexibility | When your build needs a gap-filler |

<Callout type="info" title="Build Rule">
A good Blessing set should make your best move stronger and your worst situation less dangerous. If an upgrade does neither, skip it.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Boss Prep and Survival Checks">

Bosses in Solo Leveling KARMA reward pattern recognition more than reckless damage. The earlier you learn how a boss controls space, the less likely you are to panic during later phases. Before each major fight, make sure your build has one answer for burst damage, one answer for distance, and one answer for recovery.

| Boss | Main Pressure | What to Prepare | Safer Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cerberus | Wide-area fire and heavy melee | Mobility and spacing | Keep moving and punish openings |
| Hobgoblin Leader | Adds and close-range pressure | Crowd control | Clear support units first |
| Avaricious Vulcan | Large damage bursts | Defensive timing | Avoid greedy extended trades |
| Demonic King Baran | High-level attack chains | Burst windows and patience | Hit only after clear tells |
| Statue of Cartenon Temple | Massive area threat | Survival and reaction speed | Prioritize movement over greed |

| Failure Trigger | What Usually Causes It | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Overcommitting | Staying in melee too long | Reset spacing sooner |
| Poor resource timing | Using burst without a window | Save power for safe openings |
| Weak crowd control | Ignoring adds and summons | Bring one control answer |
| Panic movement | Burning dodges early | Move with a plan |

<Callout type="success" title="Boss Rule">
If you can learn one boss pattern per run, your progress speeds up quickly. Clean execution beats forcing damage into unsafe windows.
</Callout>

</Section>
