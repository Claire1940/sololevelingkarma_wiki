<Section icon={Target} title="Solo Leveling KARMA boss fight strategy, step by step">

A strong boss plan is simple: enter with a stable build, learn the rhythm, then spend damage only when the arena is safe. This is the part where most players lose runs. They overcommit during the first good opening, then get clipped by the next pattern.

<Callout type="success" title="Best Practice">
Treat every phase transition like a reset point. Re-establish spacing, recover your rhythm, and only then push damage again.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with observation">
    Spend the first seconds reading movement speed, combo length, and AoE timing. Do not chase damage before you know the pattern.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend mobility wisely">
    Use dodges and reposition tools only when the boss commits to a real threat. Random movement makes timing harder, not easier.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Attack in short bursts">
    Take one clean combo, then disengage. If you can safely add a follow-up, do it only after the recovery window is obvious.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Stabilize during pressure spikes">
    When adds, fire zones, or wide attacks appear, switch from damage mode to survival mode immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Close the fight with discipline">
    The final stretch is where greedy plays fail most often. Keep the same pace you used at low HP and finish with control.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Common Mistake | Why It Fails | Better Habit |
|---|---|---|
| Healing too late | Leaves no recovery window | Heal before the next major hit |
| Chasing every opening | Causes trade-heavy damage | Use only guaranteed windows |
| Ignoring adds | Creates extra pressure | Clear support enemies first |
| Spamming dodges | Drains your escape tools | Dodge only on committed attacks |

If your build leans toward melee, you need stricter timing. If it leans toward ranged or summon support, you gain a little more breathing room, but the same rule still applies: survive the boss’s pattern first, then cash in on the opening.

</Section>
